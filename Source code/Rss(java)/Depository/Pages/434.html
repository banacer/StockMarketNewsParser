Ampal ousts Novik over alleged breach of fiduciary duties

Ampal's board found that Nimrod Novik threatened, solely for personal gain, to damage the company in a dispute with CEO Yosef Maiman.

6 September 11 16:43, Globes' correspondent
Ampal-American Israel Corporation (Nasdaq: AMPL; TASE:AMPL) has fired director Dr. Nimrod Novik, effective immediately, over a breach of fiduciary duties to the company and its shareholders based on various acts and omissions.

Ampal's board of directors found that Novik threatened, solely for personal gain, to damage the company in a separate dispute (to which the company is not a party) with Ampal president and CEO Yosef Maiman. Ampal added that it will use all appropriate legal measures required to protect the company from any damage caused or that will be caused by Novik and to fully recover any damages from him.

Ampal's share price fell 2.5% on the TASE today to NIS 1.59, and will open at $0.47 on Nasdaq, giving a market cap of $26.4m
