5 Stocks To Post Gains Despite Economic Uncertainty

by: Brian Nichols September 6, 2011  |  includes: COOL, DFS, KR, LULU, RIMM

August was horrible, with the first 10 days posting a loss of more than 11% on the Dow Jones. The second half of the month we saw light and began to trend higher, which gave investors a reason to be optimistic about the future. Yet September has started rough. After considering what's ahead, I am no longer optimistic about the start of the new month. Throughout September, there will be several important announcements which could affect the trend of the markets or push the major indices lower. Below is a look at a few key dates, what they could mean for the market, and five stocks that could see gains despite the trend.

I am expecting more losses from the markets during September, as there are several key events scattered throughout the month, each presenting a strong chance to move the markets lower if investors hear something unpleasant.

  • Sept. 8: President Obama speaks on jobs and unveils a proposal to create new jobs. This is not what I expect to drive the markets lower, although it could. I expect Congress' reaction will drive the markets lower as I expect it to be reluctant to agree to any plan that will increase spending. If the market believes that Congress may not approve the plan or if the markets believe Obama's plan is unrealistic, it could drive the markets lower as investors may believe that Washington is running out of options.
  • Sept. 15: Greece needs more aid. Greece has arguably the most significant financial crisis in Europe, which plays a large role in its overall financial health. Investors are hopeful that Greece receives aid quickly and without any problems. A large portion of the loss experienced in early August was related to the European debt crisis as high sovereign debt affected the stock of American banks. Financial institutions across America posted losses with the same intensity as European banks, despite being completely different institutions.
  • Sept. 20: All eyes will be on Ben Bernanke, once again, as investors are still hopeful the chairman will implement some type of quantitative easing. Bernanke spoke in Jackson Hole last month, insinuating that federal aid was being explored and could potentially come in the future. I do not believe that investors will get anything positive out of this speech. Bernanke was correct on his analysis of the economy as large corporations are posting strong earnings and regardless of speed, the economy is growing.
  • Sept. 30: The short-sale ban for many European financial stocks will be discussed and possibly lifted for the countries that implemented the regulation. I do not believe the ban will be lifted as the countries are in no position to open the banks to more loss. However, I do not believe the ban has been successful, and further reflects an act of desperation. Despite its relevance, if the ban is lifted, I believe it will have a strong impact on financial institutions in America since our banks trade heavily on global financial news. I cannot imagine U.S. markets trending higher if the ban is lifted, regardless of its meaning.

These events are almost perfectly positioned throughout the month to cause potential distress within the markets. Investors have a pessimistic view as the market is cautious to post large gains on positive developments but trends very low on news with little importance. This reflects our consumer confidence levels, which are at lowest levels in several years, as investors are not confident, in our markets or economy.

Getting to the Point

Despite uncertainty, there are a few potential bright spots within the market during September. Several companies will release earnings during the month and present the likelihood of outperforming previous years earnings by a large margin. Below is a brief look at five stocks and why I believe each could see gains, as a result of earnings, despite economic uncertainty.

Lululemon Athletica (LULU) will be announce earnings on Sept. 9. The company is experiencing a high level of growth as its yoga-inspired fitness apparel has become increasingly popular among women across North America. During the most recent quarter, the company posted revenue of $186.8 million, a gain of 35%, and net income of $33.4 million, a gain of more than 70% year-over-year. The company has released guidance with revenue of $200-205 million for the upcoming quarter. Below is an unlinked chart of the company's last five earnings, from Reuters, which shows the company has outperformed EPS four of the last five times with one quarter on the high end of expectations. The company has consistently given guidance and outperformed its own guidance; I expect this quarter to be no different with the stock posting large gains after earnings are released.

The Kroger Company (KR) will announce earnings on Sept. 9. The stock is currently trading near the same price as it was three months ago when it announced last quarter earnings. The stock posted gains of more than 5% after posting strong earnings and a solid outlook for the future. I expect the second quarter to be similar as the company has now posted 30 consecutive quarters of increased sales along with four of the last five quarters exceeding EPS expectations, which can be viewed on the chart below. I anticipate the company to continue the trend of increased sales along with beating expectations which should provide moderate gains as the stock is priced near the middle of its 52-week range.

Majestic Entertainment Company (COOL) is a small cap stock expected to announced earnings on September 12. This stock has posted yearly gains of 250% as sales from the best-selling video game Zumba, among others, sent the company to a new level of profitability. However, since the company announced earnings on June 14, the stock has fallen 45% despite 300% growth in revenue year-over-year along with strong income. With the stock price so low I am especially optimistic of its reaction to the upcoming earnings report. The stock has posted gains of 16% over the last month as news of Zumba 2 and Parking Wars 2 gave investors a reason to be excited. Earnings should be significantly stronger than last year and I expect guidance to increase with the upcoming sequels being released. With the price so low there's no reason the reaction from the market will not be positive as this company continues to grow with no debt on its balance sheet.

Research in Motion (RIMM) will announce earnings on Sept. 15. The stock has lost more than 45% of its value year-to-date as investors fear the company has lost its edge over competitors. The stock has gained over 25% during the last month as a bottom for the stock has been reached allowing the stock to trend higher to appropriate levels. I expect controversy surrounding this company's upcoming earnings report as investors have strong opinions regarding the company with both sides presenting valid arguments. The company lowered full year and second quarter guidance after the first quarter earnings were released, which further added to the company's downtrend. I believe the loss was exaggerated, which could explain the recent gains from the company's stock. I also believe this stock presents a strong possibility to post large gains following its earnings for two reasons. The company released new handset products, not too long ago, pricing the products very competitively along with older models being offered at prices of $0.01 or free with a subscription to a service provider. Regardless of how some investors, or consumers, feel about the Blackberry products, many consumers prefer Blackberry and there are many more who will take advantage of its cheap price. I expect for sales during this quarter to be substantially high since the company did not compete with a new Apple (AAPL) device at the time of its release. The second reason can be seen by looking at the below chart. The company has done a great job at setting realistic goals that can be achieved and surpassed as RIMM has posted EPS that either exceeded or posted towards the high end of guidance each of the last five quarters. I expect this quarter to be no different as I am certain the company will meet guidance and possibly exceed as I expect new phone sales to be substantially higher, with new products released. Therefore, I anticipate the stock posting substantial gains as it's currently trading with a price to earnings under 5.0 along with decent forward looking statements as new products are set to arrive later in the year.

Discover Financial Services (DFS) will announce earnings on Sept. 19. Despite trading as part of the financial sector, the stock has posted yearly gains of nearly 53%. During the most recent quarter, the company announced all-time record quarterly earnings of $600 million or $1.09 a share. This financial stock continues to impress by posting earnings that exceed expectations on a regular basis, which can be seen below. I believe the stock is a strong buy, both before earnings for short term gains and long term for several years of large gains. I am confident this company will surpass expectations, as it's done consistently, and will offer large gains to investors who hold through earnings. The stock is trading near the same price as it was before last earnings report, which was followed by the stock reaching 52-week highs and posting large gains. I anticipate the stock posting gains from this point forward as more investors will buy to profit from the company's growth, and with a price to earnings of 8 it just adds to the reasons to take advantage of this fast growing undervalued stock.
