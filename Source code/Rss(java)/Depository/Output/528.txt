Grupo Financiero Galicia S.A. (NASDAQ: GGAL) was downgraded by equities
research analysts at Raymond James (NYSE: RJF) from an “outperform”
rating to a “market perform” rating in a research note issued to
investors on Tuesday.

Separately, analysts at Zacks Investment Research downgraded shares of
Grupo Financiero Galicia S.A. from an “outperform” rating to a “neutral”
rating in a research note to investors on Wednesday, August 31st.

Shares of Grupo Financiero Galicia S.A. opened at 11.20 on Wednesday.
Grupo Financiero Galicia S.A. has a 52 week low of $8.36 and a 52 week
high of $17.10. The stock’s 50-day moving average is $13.04 and its
200-day moving average is $13.14. The company has a market cap of $1.390
billion and a price-to-earnings ratio of 7.42.
