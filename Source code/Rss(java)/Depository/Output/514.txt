Wall Street To Open Sharply Lower On Global Worries; Hot Stocks: BAC,
JPM, C, PAY, SUN


By: iStockAnalyst  | Sep 06, 2011 | Stocks: AES, BAC, C, FCEL, JPM, PAY,
SUN,

U.S. stock futures pointed to a sharply lower open on Wall Street Tuesday
as investors return from the long Labor Day weekend to face European
turmoil and lingering disappointment over last week's jobs data.

On the economic front, the Institute for Supply Management will release
its index of activity for the U.S. services sector at 10:00 a.m. EST.

Ahead of the opening bell, Dow industrial average futures were trading
lower by 219 point, or 1.95 percent, to 10,989. Nasdaq 100 futures were
down 39 points, or 1.80 percent, to 2,125.75. Standard & Poor's 500
futures were trading lower by 26 points, or 2.22 percent, to 1,143.30,
today.

Hot Stocks of the Day: BAC, JPM, C, FCEL, PAY, SUN, AES

Bank of America Corp. (NYSE: BAC) was trading lower by 3.59 percent to
$6.99, JPMorgan Chase & Co. (NYSE: JPM) was down 2.51 percent to $33.76,
and Citigroup Inc. (NYSE: C) was down 3.31 percent to $27.46 in
pre-market trading Tuesday. The three banks are part of the group of 17
being sued by the U.S. to recoup $196 billion spent on mortgage-backed
securities sold to Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac.

FuelCell Energy Inc. (Nasdaq: FCEL) is scheduled to report its third
quarter FY 2011 financial results after the closing bell Tuesday.
Analysts expect FuelCell to report a loss of $0.09 per share on revenue
of $28.89 million. FCEL last traded at $1.04, down 5.45 percent on Friday.

Verifone Systems Inc. (NYSE: PAY) is scheduled to report its third
quarter FY 2011 earnings results after the closing bell Tuesday. Analysts
expect the company to report earnings of $0.46 per share on revenue of
$299.40 million. PAY is trading lower by 0.14 percent to $34.75 in
pre-market trading.

Sunoco Inc. (NYSE: SUN) said Tuesday it plans to exit its refining
business and will record a pretax charge of between $1.9 billion and $2.2
billion in the third quarter. SUN is trading higher by 1.08 percent to
$36.50 in pre-market trading today.

AES Corp. (NYSE: AES) could be in focus today after the company said that
its chief executive officer Paul Hanrahan will step down as of September
30 and will be succeeded in the post and on the board by Chief Operating
Officer Andres Gluski. AES last traded at $10.32, down 3.01 percent on
Friday.

Global Markets: FTSE, CAC40, DAX, Nikkei 225, Straits Times, Hang Seng,
Shanghai Composite Index, BSE 30

Asian markets closed mixed today. Among the gainers were Hong Kong's
Hang, up 94.10 points, or 0.48 percent, to 19,710.50; Singapore's Straits
Times, up 1.16 points, or 0.04 percent, to close at 2,774.33; and India's
BSE 30, up 149.48 points, or 0.89 percent, to 16,862.81. Among the
decliners were Japan's Nikkei 225, down 193.89 points, or 2.21 percent,
to 8,590.57; and China's Shanghai Composite Index, down 8.21 points, or
0.33 percent to 2,470.52.

The European indexes were trading higher mid-day Tuesday. Britain's FTSE
100 was trading higher at 5,145.94, adding 43.36 points, or 0.85 percent.
Germany's DAX was trading up by 17.65 points, or 0.34 percent, to
5,263.83. France's CAC40 was trading lower by 5.31 points, or 0.18
percent, to 3,004.85.

Oil falls near $84 mark on global slowdown fears

Ahead of the opening bell, crude oil was lower by 2.05 points, or 2.37
percent, to $84.40 per barrel. Gold stood at 1,879.40, up 5.70 points, or
0.30 percent, on Tuesday.

In the currency market, the euro was trading higher by 0.30 percent to
1.4137 against the U.S. dollar. The dollar was trading higher at
Â¥77.2650, up 0.41 percent against the Japanese yen. The British pound
is trading lower by 0.16 percent to $1.6085 against the dollar on Tuesday.

On Friday, Wall Street closed lower. The Dow Jones industrial average
closed down by 253.31 points, or 2.20 percent, to settle at 11,240.26.
The Nasdaq ended lower by 65.71 points, or 2.58 percent, at 2,480.33. The
Standard & Poor's 500 ended down by 30.45 points, or 2.53 percent, at
1,173.97.
